# Getting around

Quick ways to navigate Boulder without stress.

## Quick version
- Walkable downtown; Pearl Street is pedestrian-friendly.
- Bike lanes and multi-use paths cover much of the city.
- Rideshare and taxis are plentiful; set your pin on the corner for easier pickup.
- RTD buses connect to Denver, DIA, and local spots.
- Parking tight near Pearl/Chautauqua—use garages or arrive early.
- Winter storms: give yourself extra time and wear traction shoes.

## By foot or bike
- Boulder Creek Path and Broadway/28th have bike lanes; e-bikes are widely available to rent.
- Lock bikes securely and avoid overnight street parking when possible.
- Many trailheads are reachable by bike, including routes toward Chautauqua and Sanitas.

## Transit & rideshare
- RTD SKIP on Broadway and the HOP loop the core areas; pay with the RTD app or cash.
- AB1/AB2 buses go to Denver International Airport from downtown and Table Mesa Park-n-Ride.
- Rideshare pickup is easiest on corners away from Pearl Street Mall crowds.

## Parking tips
- Downtown garages show availability signs; first hour often discounted.
- Chautauqua and popular trailheads can fill by mid-morning—consider early starts or transit.
- In winter, watch for snow routes and plow signage before parking overnight.
